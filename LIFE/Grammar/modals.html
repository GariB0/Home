1. Can, Could

Can

We use can to say that something is possible or allowed, or that somebody has the ability to do something.
We use can + infinitve
But can has only wo forms: Can & Could (present and past)


* the negative is can't

be able to

You can say that somebody is able to do something, but can is more usual.

Could

Sometimes could is the past of can. We use could especially with:
see hear smell taste feel remember unmderstand
We also use "could" to say that somebody had the general ability or permission to do something.

In a specific situation

We use "could" for general ability in a specific situation that somebody did something.
was/were able to or manged to


* the negative is couldn't is possible in all situations.

Could in a number of ways

We use could for actions that are not realisitc.
We also use "could" to say that something is possible now or in the furture.
We use "could have done" to talk about the past.
Something could have happened = it was possible but did not happen.
We also use "couldn't" to say that something would not be possible.

* For the past we use "couldn't have done"
